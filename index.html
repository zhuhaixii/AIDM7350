Travel Itinerary

Day 1 | Hong Kong Island Classic Tour

Morning: Victoria Peak (Peak Tram + The Peak Tower, panoramic view of Victoria Harbour)

Afternoon: Central Market → Mid-Levels Escalator → Causeway Bay (Shopping & Sightseeing)

Evening: Victoria Harbour (Symphony of Lights Show)

Day 2 | Disneyland Fairy Tale Tour

All Day: Hong Kong Disneyland Resort (7 themed areas, fireworks show is a must-see)

Tips: Download the Disneyland app in advance and get a Fastpass

Day 3 | Kowloon Cultural Tour

Morning: Tsim Sha Tsui (Avenue of Stars + Harbour City) → Star Ferry (cross Victoria Harbour)

Afternoon: Wong Tai Sin Temple (Pray for blessings) → Yau Ma Tei Temple Street (Local culture)

Evening: Mong Kok (Portland Street + Ladies' Market, try local snacks)

Day 4 | Outlying Island Leisure Tour

Morning: Central Pier → Cheung Chau Island (30 mins by ferry, coastal scenery)

Afternoon: Cycling on Cheung Chau → Seafood Feast → Return (Buy souvenirs in Central)

Top Attractions

Victoria Peak

Hong Kong's landmark, take the century-old Peak Tram to the top and enjoy 360° views of the island and harbour

Victoria Harbour

One of the world's three natural harbours, don't miss the Symphony of Lights at 8 pm every night

Hong Kong Disneyland

The world's smallest Disneyland with exclusive attractions, full of fairy-tale atmosphere

Cheung Chau Island

Car-free outlying island, enjoy cycling, fresh seafood and slow Hong Kong life by the sea

Local Cuisine

Classic Cha Chaan Teng Dishes

  • Pineapple Bun with Butter + Iced Lemon Tea (Hong Kong afternoon tea classic)
  • Braised Pork Rice with Soft-boiled Egg (Rich sauce, tender pork)
  • Wonton Noodles (Fresh shrimp wontons, chewy bamboo noodles)
  • French Toast + Hong Kong-style Milk Tea

Street Snacks

  • Curry Fish Balls (Chewy and flavorful, a Hong Kong staple)
  • Egg Waffles (Crispy outside, soft inside, original or chocolate flavor)
  • Wan Chai Congee + Fried Pork Intestines
  • Water Chestnut Pudding (Sweet and Q-bouncy, childhood memory)

Seafood & Cantonese Cuisine

  • Typhoon Shelter Fried Crab (Hong Kong signature, garlicky and crispy)
  • Salt and Pepper Mantis Shrimp (Plump meat, rich flavor)
  • Roasted Meat Rice (Roast goose/duck/pork combo)
  • Seafood Hot Pot (Must-try in Cheung Chau/Sai Kung, fresh and sweet)

Travel Tips

Currency & Payment

HKD is widely used; WeChat/Alipay are accepted in malls/restaurants. Prepare cash for street snacks

Transportation

Octopus Card (for MTR/bus/convenience stores); Star Ferry is the cost-effective way to cross the harbour

Language

Cantonese, Mandarin and English are all usable; staff in scenic spots/malls speak fluent Mandarin

Business Hours

Malls: 10:00-22:00; Cha Chaan Teng: 7:00-23:00; Street snacks: open till midnight

Clothing & Luggage

Humid climate, light clothing all year round; bring a thin coat for air-conditioned malls/MTR

Internet & Communication

Activate Hong Kong/Macau roaming or buy a local SIM card; free WiFi in all scenic spots/malls
